British/Colombian artist Matthew Plummer-Fernandez works across sculpture, print, software, and installation. After receiving an MA from London's Royal College of Art in 2009, he completed his practice-based doctorate at Goldsmiths, University of London, in 2019. Plummer-Fernandez's interests in copyleft culture, digital fabrication, and social-computational entanglements, interrelate to form a varied body of work that is influenced by the artistic traditions of Generative Art, Critical Design, and Internet Art. His more recent works further borrow from Regenerative Design.

Plummer-Fernandez's work has been exhibited extensively, and commissioned by institutions including Somerset House, London, ZKM, Karlruhe, AND Festival, Manchester, and the Victoria & Albert Museum, London. His work is in the collection of the Centre Pompidou in Paris, and received an award of distinction at Ars Electronica, in 2014. Plummer-Fernandez is represented by Nome gallery, Berlin, and is currently an associate lecturer at the University of Arts, London, and a visiting researcher at Goldsmiths.

In his spare time, Plummer-Fernandez is an agroecological gardener, environmental activist, musician, and plays Situationist 3-sided football.
