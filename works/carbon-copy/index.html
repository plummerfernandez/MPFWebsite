Carbon Copy

2019
FOSS (free open-source software), open-source 3D files, willow, PLA


Carbon Copy is an ambitious project of many inter-related works – a Gesamtkunstwerk – currently in progress. The body of work will consist of drawings, renders, designs, research, software, digital-commons contributions, ecology, and lived situations. A unifying thread that runs through Carbon Copy is a cultural intervention into the carbon cycle, however the total work cannot simply be reduced to a singular concept and is best understood as a multiplicity. For now, an overall summary of the project can be described as:

Carbon Copy is an experimental manifestation of an imagined activity that sees humans cultivating, building and sharing open source structures that by design, rebalance the carbon cycle at multiple points. These structures act as carbon sinks since their principle material is fast-growing and regenerative willow rods, that rapidly transform CO2 into biomass. The growing of willow also sits within rewilding efforts to restore wild habitats, as willow is the second most beneficial tree to other species, second to oak. The assembly of the willow rods into useable structures copies the formation of carbon nanotubes –arrangements of carbon atoms with incredible physical properties. These structures, when recreated on a human-scale, and larger, become vertical pillars upon which to elevate photovoltaics and wind power, whilst also supporting climbing plant-life, insects and birds, or to be used as communal space for growing plant-based food vertically. The towers also work as symbols – monoliths of the carbon age. The designs of these willow structures are distributed as software and 3D-print files contributed to the digital-commons, for all to copy. The project will manifest as both visual propositions and as a live experiment involving a network of participants and collaborators.









“Despair is the state we fall into when our imagination fails. When we have no stories that describe the present and the guide the future, hope evaporates. Without a new story, a story that is positive and propositional rather than reactive and oppositional, nothing changes. With such a story, everything changes.”

- George Monbiot, Out of the Wreckage: A new politics for an age of crisis, 2017.

The carbon cycle is the biogeochemical cycle by which carbon is exchanged between air, earth, oceans and organic matter – the four reservoirs of carbon. The industrial age, and burning of fossil fuels in particular, has altered the balance between these reservoirs, transferring too much carbon to the air, in the gaseous form of CO2 – a process that now alarmingly threatens life on Earth by causing increasingly unsafe levels of global warming. Various societal actions can mitigate this – dropping fossil fuels, flying less, consuming less, switching to wind and solar energy, planting more trees, rewilding, and moving towards plant-based diets and agroecological approaches to growing food. These combined activities require deep shifts in worldviews and cultural attitudes that many may find hard, however, bold new stories and ideas (alongside a sense of urgency and emergency) can help shift minds to adopt and develop transformative changes in thinking and living. Carbon Copy is a reframing of existing solutions into an audacious new story, one that is “positive and propositional” as Monbiot suggests, and bridges various carbon-rebalancing activities into one multi-layered societal activity. It is crafted to be compelling, inviting and quick to adopt, through visual narratives, open-sourcing, and community-led implementation, inspired by post-capitalist movements such as Incredible Edible, Transition Network and Extinction Rebellion. Carbon Copy is what anthropologist Lucy Suchman would describe as a “cultural imaginary” – a collective vision for the future, that is made manifest through developments in science, technology and culture.







Salix Viminalis (osier willow) are being grown from cuttings to eventually produce a supply of willow rods for Carbon Copy. Communities will be able to share this resource by taking cuttings of their plants to pass on to others.






Parametric software tool for designing carbon tubes, created in Processing in collaboration with Frederic Van Houte (wblut).




First and rather small proof-of-concept to test the design and assembly. The assembly has been designed to be straightforward and easy to replicate without any complex machinery, apart from a cheap home 3D printer. The willow is cut and shaped by hand with secateurs and pencil sharpeners.
