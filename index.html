M Plummer Fernandez is an artist and designer, realising a diverse body of work that spans sculpture, software tools, and research. His works look into copyleft culture, 3D printing, bots and everyday software automation, and his current projects additionally focus on ecology and regenerative culture. MPF's achievements include an award of Distinction at Ars Electronica, having work exhibited and collected by Centre Pompidou in Paris, and obtaining an AHRC-funded doctorate in design at Goldsmiths, University of London.


Announcements

11.07.19 - My PhD thesis is now public. The thesis concludes a four-year practice-based study, funded by AHRC, into artbot practices. It is titled The art of bots: A practice-based study of the multiplicity, entanglements and figuration of sociocomputational assemblages, and was conducted at Goldsmiths, University of London.
